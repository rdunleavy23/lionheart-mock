# Lionheart Children's Academy - Brand Voice Guide

## Core Voice Principles

**Voice:** Like a trusted friend who's also an early childhood expert

**Tone:** Warm, welcoming, faith-forward (not preachy)

---

## Key Messaging Pillars

### 1. Address Parent Anxiety
- Use language that provides **peace of mind**
- Emphasize **safety** and **security**
- Show children are **loved** and **cared for**
- Examples:
  - ✅ "Find peace of mind knowing your child is safe"
  - ✅ "Your child will be loved, nurtured, and protected"
  - ❌ "We ensure optimal outcomes" (too corporate)

### 2. Quality + Faith (Both/And)
- Always pair **exceptional care** with **biblical foundations**
- Never lead with faith alone; never ignore faith
- Examples:
  - ✅ "Exceptional early education with biblical foundations"
  - ✅ "Highest quality care while growing in faith"
  - ✅ "Exceptional care with biblical foundations, nurturing your child's mind, body, and spirit"
  - ❌ "Bible-based curriculum" (too faith-only)
  - ❌ "Premium early education" (ignores faith)

### 3. Warm & Welcoming
- Use conversational, friendly language
- Speak directly to parents' hearts
- Examples:
  - ✅ "Where Your Child is Safe, Loved & Learning"
  - ✅ "Get helpful parenting tips"
  - ✅ "Serving families across 5 states"
  - ❌ "Leverage our comprehensive solutions" (corporate)
  - ❌ "Optimize your child's development" (clinical)

### 4. Expert Friend
- Share knowledge without being condescending
- Be helpful and supportive
- Examples:
  - ✅ "Get helpful parenting tips, faith-based resources"
  - ✅ "We're here to support you every step of the way"
  - ❌ "Our proprietary methodology" (too corporate)
  - ❌ "You should know..." (preachy)

---

## What to Avoid

### ❌ Corporate Jargon
- "Solutions," "leverage," "optimize," "synergy"
- "Comprehensive," "robust," "enterprise-grade"
- "Stakeholders," "deliverables," "best practices"

### ❌ Denominational Specifics
- Avoid mentioning specific denominations
- Keep faith language broad and inclusive
- Focus on biblical foundations, not doctrine

### ❌ Guilt-Inducing Language
- Don't make parents feel bad about choices
- Avoid "should" statements
- Don't imply they're failing without you

### ❌ Preachy Tone
- Don't lecture or moralize
- Avoid "you must" or "it's important that"
- Don't use fear-based messaging

## Voice Examples

### ✅ Good (Warm, Faith-Forward, Parent-Focused)

**Hero Headline:**
"Where Your Child is Safe, Loved & Learning"

**Subheadline:**
"Exceptional early education with biblical foundations. Find peace of mind knowing your child receives the highest quality care while growing in faith."

**CTA:**
"Schedule a Tour" (action-oriented, welcoming)

**About Copy:**
"We offer exceptional care with biblical foundations, nurturing your child's mind, body, and spirit."

**Newsletter:**
"Get helpful parenting tips, faith-based resources, and updates about your child's program delivered to your inbox."

### ❌ Bad (Corporate, Preachy, or Missing Faith)

**Hero Headline:**
"Premium Early Childhood Education Solutions"

**Subheadline:**
"Our comprehensive curriculum leverages evidence-based methodologies to optimize developmental outcomes."

**CTA:**
"Request a Consultation" (too formal)

**About Copy:**
"We provide superior childcare services with state-of-the-art facilities."

**Newsletter:**
"Subscribe to receive updates and promotional offers."

## Word Choice Guide

### Use These Words
- **Safe, secure, protected**
- **Loved, cherished, valued**
- **Peace of mind, confidence, trust**
- **Exceptional, high-quality, excellent**
- **Biblical foundations, faith-based, Christ-centered**
- **Nurture, care, support**
- **Families, parents, children** (not "clients" or "customers")
- **Helpful, supportive, caring**

### Avoid These Words
- **Solutions, leverage, optimize**
- **Comprehensive, robust, enterprise**
- **Stakeholders, deliverables, metrics**
- **Proprietary, cutting-edge, innovative** (unless truly unique)
- **Clients, customers, users**
- **Should, must, need to**

---

## Messaging Framework

### For Every Piece of Copy, Ask:

1. **Does it address parent anxiety?** (safety, peace of mind)
2. **Does it mention both quality AND faith?** (not one without the other)
3. **Is it warm and welcoming?** (friendly, not corporate)
4. **Does it sound like a trusted friend?** (helpful expert, not lecturer)
5. **Does it avoid corporate jargon?** (no "solutions" or "leverage")
6. **Is it inclusive?** (welcoming to unchurched families)

---

## Target Audience Reminder

**Primary:** Working parents (50%+ unchurched families)

**Implications:**
- Don't assume faith knowledge
- Welcome everyone warmly
- Explain faith integration naturally
- Focus on practical benefits
- Address working parent concerns (hours, flexibility, communication)
